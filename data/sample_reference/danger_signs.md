# Danger Signs During Pregnancy

## EMERGENCY — Immediately transport to hospital

### Bleeding during pregnancy
Any vaginal bleeding during pregnancy is a danger sign. Transport to nearest hospital/FRU immediately.

### Severe anemia with breathlessness
Hemoglobin below 7 g/dL with breathlessness requires immediate referral for possible blood transfusion.

### Headache with blurred vision and fits
Combination of severe headache, blurred vision, and/or convulsions indicates possible eclampsia. Refer to FRU immediately.

### Swelling all over the body
Generalized edema (not just feet) especially with headache or visual disturbances suggests pre-eclampsia.

### Convulsions/seizures
Any seizure during pregnancy is a medical emergency. Do not delay transport.

### Reduced or absent fetal movement
If baby has stopped moving or moves much less than usual, especially in 3rd trimester, immediate medical evaluation needed.

### Preterm labour
Labour pains or water breaking before 37 weeks requires immediate referral.

### High fever during pregnancy
Fever above 38.5°C during pregnancy or within 1 month of delivery.

## Contact PHC for:
- Burning micturition
- High-grade fever or any medical illness
- Vaginal bleeding in early pregnancy
- Excessive nausea and vomiting
- Leaking before onset of labour
- High blood pressure detected in ANC
- Fainting and/or pain in abdomen

## Contact FRU for:
- Vaginal bleeding in advanced pregnancy
- Decreased or no fetal movements
- Swelling all over body with palpitations
- Swelling with blurring of vision
- High blood pressure in ANC
- Fits/convulsions
- Labour pains or leaking before 9 months
- Leaking for more than 12 hours without labour
- Foul smelling discharge
- Labour pains lasting more than 12 hours
- Continuous abdominal pain
