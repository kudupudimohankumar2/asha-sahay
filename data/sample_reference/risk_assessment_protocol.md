# Risk Assessment Protocol for Pregnancy

## Risk Categories

### EMERGENCY (Immediate referral required)
- Vaginal bleeding at any stage
- Severe anemia: Hemoglobin < 7 g/dL
- Severe hypertension: BP > 160/110 mmHg
- Eclampsia: Convulsions/seizures
- Reduced or absent fetal movement
- Severe headache with blurred vision
- Preterm labour (before 37 weeks)
- Premature rupture of membranes

### HIGH RISK (Specialist follow-up needed)
- Pregnancy-induced hypertension: BP > 140/90 mmHg
- Gestational diabetes: Fasting glucose > 126 mg/dL
- Adolescent pregnancy: Age < 18 years
- Advanced maternal age: Age > 35 years
- Previous C-section or stillbirth
- High fever during pregnancy
- Excessive edema with other signs
- Multiple pregnancy

### ELEVATED (Increased monitoring)
- Moderate anemia: Hemoglobin 7-10 g/dL
- Urine protein positive
- Excessive weight gain or loss
- History of pregnancy complications
- Mild hypertension trending upward

### NORMAL
- All vital signs within normal range
- No risk factors identified
- Normal fetal movement
- Adequate weight gain

## Assessment Frequency
- NORMAL: Standard ANC schedule (4-7 visits)
- ELEVATED: Biweekly monitoring from 28 weeks
- HIGH RISK: Weekly monitoring from 28 weeks, specialist consultation
- EMERGENCY: Immediate referral, continuous monitoring

## Normal Ranges During Pregnancy
- Hemoglobin: 11-15 g/dL (below 11 is anemia)
- Blood Pressure: 90/60 to 120/80 mmHg
- Fasting Blood Sugar: 70-95 mg/dL
- Fetal Heart Rate: 120-160 bpm
- Weight Gain: 9-11 kg total, 1 kg/month in last 6 months
